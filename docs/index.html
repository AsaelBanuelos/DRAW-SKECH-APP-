Step 1 — Grid (Proportion Transfer)
Draw this grid lightly on your paper. Copy one square at a time. Use the center lines to anchor key features (eyes, nose).
Step 2 — Sketch (Contour Lines)
Light pressure (HB/2H). Trace main contours only — don't detail yet. Focus on accurate shapes and placement.
Step 3 — Edges (Hard / Medium / Soft)
Hard edges (red) = sharp pencil, firm strokes. Medium (yellow) = controlled. Soft (blue) = blend with stump. This is what makes realism.
Step 4 — Shading (Value Zones & Light)
Lay in 4 value zones from light to dark. Follow the arrow for light direction. Build layers gradually — darks last.
Step 5 — Values (Full Tonal Map)
Compare your drawing to this 5-level map. Squint to check: are darks dark enough? Lights preserved? Adjust until they match.
